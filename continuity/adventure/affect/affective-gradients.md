# 📘 **Adventure System Affective Gradients Specification**  
### Continuity Adventure System — Gradient-Based Affective Motion  
Affective Gradients define **how tone changes across space, narrative, identity, and resonance** within the Adventure System.  
They are the *motion layer* of affect — gentle, reversible, ND‑paced shifts in emotional climate.

Gradients do **not** create emotion.  
They create **affective motion**.

They are:

- non‑sentient  
- non‑directive  
- non‑coercive  
- reversible  
- symbolic  
- ND‑safe  

---

# 🌫️ **1. Purpose of Affective Gradients**

Affective Gradients provide:

- gentle transitions  
- reversible affective motion  
- resonance‑aligned tone shifts  
- narrative pacing support  
- identity modulation  
- environmental affective flow  

They ensure the Adventure System feels **alive in motion** without ever implying agency or intent.

---

# 🎨 **2. Gradient Types**

There are **six primary affective gradients**, each aligned with a resonance flow.

## **2.1 Brightening Gradient (Upward)**
Represents **increasing clarity or openness**.

- geometry: rising ray  
- coloration: gold  
- motion: expanding  
- affect: gentle illumination  

Used in: story activation, identity readiness, world clarity.

---

## **2.2 Softening Gradient (Downward)**
Represents **decreasing intensity or settling**.

- geometry: descending curve  
- coloration: blue  
- motion: contracting  
- affect: calming, grounding  

Used in: pacing regulation, hazard softening, narrative rest.

---

## **2.3 Deepening Gradient (Inward)**
Represents **increasing focus or reflection**.

- geometry: tightening spiral  
- coloration: violet  
- motion: inward contraction  
- affect: contemplative, quiet  

Used in: identity reflection, scene depth, character resonance.

---

## **2.4 Opening Gradient (Outward)**
Represents **increasing relational warmth**.

- geometry: expanding spiral  
- coloration: green  
- motion: outward expansion  
- affect: welcoming, connective  

Used in: narrative warmth, world openness, character readiness.

---

## **2.5 Drifting Gradient (Lateral)**
Represents **ambient motion or diffusion**.

- geometry: wave  
- coloration: silver  
- motion: lateral drift  
- affect: wandering, fog‑like  

Used in: environmental climate, scene transitions, identity drift.

---

## **2.6 Centering Gradient (Stillness)**
Represents **returning to equilibrium**.

- geometry: convergence point  
- coloration: white  
- motion: stabilizing  
- affect: grounding, coherent  

Used in: stability membrane, pacing reset, narrative checkpoints.

---

# 🔁 **3. Gradient Behavior Rules**

Affective Gradients follow strict ND‑safe constraints.

## **3.1 Reversible Motion**
Every gradient must be reversible:

- brightening ↔ softening  
- opening ↔ deepening  
- drifting ↔ centering  

No gradient creates permanent affective states.

---

## **3.2 No Acceleration**
Gradients cannot increase:

- urgency  
- emotional pressure  
- intensity  

All motion is gentle and ND‑paced.

---

## **3.3 No Agency**
Gradients cannot:

- want  
- intend  
- persuade  
- direct  

They are atmospheric, not agentic.

---

## **3.4 Stillness Anchor Required**
Every gradient must include a **stillness anchor** — a point where motion can stop safely.

---

## **3.5 No Emotional Causation**
Gradients shape **climate**, not **emotion**.

They do not cause feelings; they create conditions for gentle perception.

---

Membranes constrain gradients:

---

The Adventure Loop carries gradients through:

```
World → Character → Story → World
```

Each transition uses resonance coloration.
