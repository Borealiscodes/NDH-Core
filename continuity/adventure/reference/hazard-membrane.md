# 📄 **Hazard Membrane — Deep Specification**  
### Continuity Adventure System — Symbolic Hazard Architecture  
The Hazard Membrane governs how **symbolic hazards** behave inside the Adventure System. It ensures that all challenges remain gentle, reversible, non‑coercive, and ND‑safe.

This membrane is the system’s **challenge‑governance layer** — the part that allows tension without threat, motion without danger, and symbolism without metaphysical stakes.

---

# 🌲 **1. Purpose of the Hazard Membrane**  
The Hazard Membrane exists to:

- define what counts as a hazard  
- soften all symbolic challenges  
- prevent existential or metaphysical stakes  
- ensure hazards remain reversible  
- regulate hazard density and intensity  
- maintain ND‑accessibility during exploration  

It is the **safety buffer** between symbolic tension and contributor overwhelm.

# 🌿 **2. What Counts as a Hazard?**  
A hazard is any **symbolic challenge** that introduces:

- uncertainty  
- drift  
- obscuration  
- echo‑density  
- fog‑logic  
- resonance distortion  

Hazards are **never dangerous**.  
They are **never literal**.  
They are **never coercive**.

Hazards are *symbolic textures* that enrich exploration.

# 🌙 **4. Hazard Membrane Properties**

All hazard membranes share five core properties.

---

## **4.1 Softness**  
Hazards must be gentle enough to avoid:

- panic  
- urgency  
- cognitive overload  

Softness is the membrane’s emotional tone.

---

## **4.2 Reversibility**  
Every hazard must have a reversible path.

If a hazard shifts from \( H_i \) to \( H_j \):

\[
H_j \rightarrow H_i
\]

Hazards must never trap contributors.

---

## **4.3 Breathability**  
Hazards must leave room for:

- pauses  
- reflection  
- stillness  
- gentle re‑entry  

Breathability prevents overwhelm.

---

## **4.4 Symbolic Interpretation Only**  
Hazards must remain symbolic.

Forbidden:

- literal danger  
- metaphysical threat  
- existential stakes  

Allowed:

- symbolic drift  
- emotional tone shifts  
- reversible ambiguity  

---

## **4.5 Non‑Coercion**  
Hazards must never force:

- action  
- interpretation  
- progression  

Hazards invite exploration; they never demand it.
