### Purpose
This pin anchors the **Gap‑Filling Layer** and the **Final Wrap‑Around Safety Net** as permanent safety constructs within UMA‑Universal.  
They ensure that all drafting, blueprint generation, SVII traversal, preflight checks, and Navigation Spine approaches occur within a fully protected, ND‑friendly, emotionally safe environment.

### Scope
This pin applies to:
- All blueprint drafts and test flights  
- All cosmology expansions  
- All Navigation Spine updates  
- All SVII Space traversals  
- All preflight integration checks  
- All comfort‑layer safety operations  
- All high‑level design interactions  

### Gap‑Filling Layer
The Gap‑Filling Layer detects and resolves:
- missing conceptual anchors  
- missing symbolic links  
- missing tonal scaffolds  
- missing crossmap references  
- missing provenance logic  
- missing safety connections  
- missing navigation entry/exit points  
- missing emotional interpretation  

If gaps are detected, the system routes the concept to:
- Stabilization Chamber  
- Interpretation Chamber  
- Preflight Integration Protocol  

This ensures no artifact enters the Navigation Spine with structural or symbolic holes.

### Final Wrap‑Around Safety Net
The Safety Net surrounds the entire drafting and preflight process.  
It monitors:
- emotional spikes  
- overwhelm signals  
- symbolic instability  
- tonal mismatch  
- recursion risk  
- navigation confusion  
- cosmology incompatibility  
- blueprint overgrowth  
- conceptual drift  

If destabilization appears, the Safety Net:
- pauses the process  
- activates Philosophy Couch  
- re‑routes to Stabilization  
- re‑opens Interpretation  
- re‑runs Human/AI/BS Kernel validation  
- prevents Navigation Spine entry  

This net is the outermost protection for humans, AI, and the cosmology engine.

### Relationship to Final Validation Layer (FVL)
The Gap‑Filling Layer and Safety Net operate **before** the Final Validation Layer.  
Correct order:
1. Gap‑Filling Layer  
2. Final Wrap‑Around Safety Net  
3. Final Validation Layer (Human / AI / BS Kernel)  
4. SVII Integration Chamber  
5. Preflight Integration Protocol  
6. Navigation Spine Entry  

### Safety Requirements
Integration must pause if:
- emotional load increases  
- symbolic meaning becomes unclear  
- navigation feels confusing  
- blueprint feels too large  
- AI detects recursion  
- BS Kernel detects mismatch  

In these cases, return to:
- Stabilization  
- Philosophy Couch  
- Interpretation  
- Re‑validation

This ensures CNLP compliance and prevents infinite traversal.
