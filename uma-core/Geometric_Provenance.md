# Geometric Provenance™
**UMA Architecture — Canonical Provenance Specification**  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** 2026‑07‑02  
**Status:** Canonical Baseline  
**Author:** Borealis S. Hedling

## Purpose
Geometric Provenance™ defines the origin, constraints, lineage, and constitutional inheritance of every geometry used within UMA. It ensures that shapes, diagrams, lattices, flows, and spatial structures are traceable, governed, and aligned with the Five‑Point Core.

Provenance prevents drift, distortion, and accidental reification. It guarantees that all UMA geometries remain functional, humane, and constitutionally anchored.

## Definition
Geometric Provenance™ is the constitutional record of:
1. **Origin** — where a geometry comes from.  
2. **Constraint** — what Core principles shaped it.  
3. **Function** — what role it serves in UMA.  
4. **Lineage** — how it relates to other geometries.  
5. **Integration** — how it enters the constitutional and engineering layers.  
6. **Amendment** — how it evolves through RFCs.  

Every geometry must have a provenance entry before it becomes canonical.

## Constitutional Basis
Geometric Provenance™ inherits the Five‑Point Core:

- **Warmth** — geometries must be humane and non‑coercive.  
- **Conscience** — geometries must respect dignity and boundaries.  
- **Awareness** — geometries must reflect contextual orientation.  
- **Truth** — geometries must preserve reality and avoid distortion.  
- **Humility** — geometries must remain provisional and non‑rigid.

These principles govern geometric creation, evaluation, and amendment.

## Engineering Basis
Geometric Provenance™ ensures that geometries:

- map correctly to load‑bearing structures  
- reflect accurate runtime flow  
- align with subsystem boundaries  
- do not imply false symmetry or false equivalence  
- remain compatible with the engineering exoskeleton

Geometries must express function, not ornament.

## Runtime Basis
Geometric Provenance™ governs how geometries enter the Imagination Runtime:

- **Generative** — geometry is proposed.  
- **Simulation** — geometry is tested.  
- **Integration** — geometry is adopted or rejected.  

Provenance ensures that runtime geometries do not bypass constitutional review.

## Provenance Record Format
Each geometry must include:

### 1. Name
The canonical name of the geometry.

### 2. Origin
Where the geometry emerged (e.g., Soft Spiral Lantern, Adventure System, UMA Core).

### 3. Constitutional Constraints
Which Core principles shaped it.

### 4. Functional Role
What the geometry does (e.g., timeline anchoring, flow mapping, subsystem bracing).

### 5. Structural Placement
Where it sits in the UMA architecture.

### 6. Lineage
Related geometries and dependencies.

### 7. Amendment History
RFCs, updates, and version changes.

### 8. Integration Status
Draft, provisional, canonical, deprecated.
