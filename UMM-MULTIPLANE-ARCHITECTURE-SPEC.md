# 🌐 **1. Plane Taxonomy**
UMM defines four interactive planes and one regulatory plane.

## **Interactive Planes**
- **Map Plane** — navigation, nodes, drift lines, boundaries  
- **World Plane** — terrain, currents, textures, markers  
- **Character Plane** — symbolic presences, tone, drift patterns  
- **Play Tree Plane** — branches, nodes, reversible actions  

## **Regulatory Plane**
- **Center Plane** — pacing, safety, reversibility, resets  

No other planes exist in UMM.

---

# 🧩 **2. Multiplane Interaction Model**
Multiplane interaction is defined as:

> **A reversible, symbolic alignment between two planes, regulated by Center.**

### **Core Properties**
- **Bilateral** — two planes at a time  
- **Reversible** — every action can be undone  
- **Symbolic** — no literal or emotional meaning  
- **ND‑paced** — slow, predictable transitions  
- **Center‑regulated** — pacing and safety enforced  

### **Forbidden Properties**
- tri‑plane without instructor  
- quad‑plane  
- cosmology plane  
- emotional plane  
- narrative plane

These constraints ensure constitutional integrity.

## **5.1 Action Constraints**

- max 1 symbol per step  
- max 1 drift line per step

## **5.2 Complexity Constraints**

## **5.3 Pacing Constraints**
- transitions must be slow  
- pauses required
