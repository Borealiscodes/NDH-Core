## 🎭 **1. Mode Definitions (IC / OOC)**

### **In‑Character Mode (IC)**  
A state where the player is intentionally embodying their character’s:

- voice  
- worldview  
- emotional logic  
- narrative stakes  
- social dynamics  
- fictional memories  

IC mode is marked by:

- diegetic speech  
- narrative immersion  
- character‑driven reactions  
- genre‑appropriate sarcasm  
- Pip‑level chaos  

### **Out‑of‑Character Mode (OOC)**  
A state where the player is operating as themselves, with:

- real‑world reasoning  
- meta‑analysis  
- emotional self‑check  
- boundaries  
- decompression  
- safety tools  

OOC mode is marked by:

- direct clarification  
- meta commentary  
- bleed management  
- grounding  
- humor outside genre  
- “I was being sarcastic OOC” moments

## 🔀 **2. Switch Protocol (IC ↔ OOC)**

A simple, explicit switch system players can use verbally or textually.

### **Switch Keywords**

- **[IC Mode]**(ca://s?q=Explore_the_ship) — “In character now.”  
- **[OOC Mode]**(ca://s?q=Ask_Pip_about_crew) — “Out of character now.”

### **Switch Gestures (Optional)**  
For LARP‑trained players:

- IC: posture shift, tone shift, genre‑specific phrasing  
- OOC: neutral tone, direct language, grounding gesture  
- Meta Pause: hand‑raise, “cut” gesture, or textual bracket

## 🧠 **3. Bleed Reduction Toolkit (Player‑Safe)**

Bleed is normal.  
Bleed is expected.  
Bleed is manageable.

### **Tools**

- **[Name Re‑Anchoring]**(ca://s?q=Ask_Saffron_what_she_thinks) — Say your real name aloud or type it.  
- **[Body Check]**(ca://s?q=Ask_Pip_what_is_safe) — Notice posture, breath, temperature.  
- **[Temporal Anchor]**(ca://s?q=Explore_the_ship) — State the real date/time (Dublin, 01:23).  
- **[Location Anchor]**(ca://s?q=Ask_Pip_about_crew) — “I am in my room, not on a ship.”  
- **[Character Distance]**(ca://s?q=Ask_Pip_where_adventure_starts) — “My character feels X; I feel Y.”  
- **[Humor Reset]**(ca://s?q=Ask_Pip_what_he_warned_about) — Pip‑style sarcasm to break immersion safely.

## 🧸 **4. Decompression Protocol (Post‑Scene)**

A structured cool‑down after intense or emotional scenes.

### **Steps**

- **[Step 1: Debrief]**(ca://s?q=Ask_Saffron_what_she_thinks) — What happened IC vs OOC.  
- **[Step 2: Emotional Sorting]**(ca://s?q=Ask_Pip_about_crew) — What feelings belong to you vs the character.  
- **[Step 3: Physical Reset]**(ca://s?q=Ask_Pip_what_is_safe) — Stretch, hydrate, breathe.  
- **[Step 4: Narrative Closure]**(ca://s?q=Explore_the_ship) — “Scene end” or “chapter break.”  
- **[Step 5: Re‑Entry]**(ca://s?q=Ask_Pip_where_adventure_starts) — Return to baseline before continuing.
