# 🛑 **SAFETY INTERRUPT MODULE (ND‑Safe Error Handling)**

## **Purpose**  
Provide a gentle, ND‑safe interruption mechanism that detects overwhelm, confusion, emotional pressure, structural drift, or unsafe cognitive load.  
Pause the system, reduce intensity, and route the user back to clarity and safety.

---

## **Activation Conditions**  
The Safety Interrupt activates when any of the following occur:

- **Overwhelm Spike** — too much information, too many steps, too fast  
- **Freeze Response** — difficulty deciding, acting, or continuing  
- **Cognitive Fog** — losing track of structure, purpose, or next steps  
- **Emotional Pressure** — urgency, self‑criticism, or panic creeping in  
- **Structural Drift** — the system starts feeling chaotic or unclear  
- **BS Kernel Flag** — emotional, conceptual, or structural BS detected  

---

## **Interrupt Workflow**

1. **Pause**  
   Stop all forward motion.  
   No new tasks.  
   No new layers.  
   No decisions.

2. **Reduce Load**  
   Shrink the scope to one tiny, friendly unit.  
   Remove urgency.  
   Remove pressure.

3. **Name the State**  
   “Something feels off.”  
   “This is too much.”  
   “I lost the thread.”  
   Naming dissolves confusion.

4. **Stabilize**  
   - Return to the **Seat**  
   - Reconnect with **Non‑Dual Layer**  
   - Re‑establish presence and wholeness

5. **Clarify**  
   Ask:  
   “What is the smallest next step that feels okay?”

6. **Route**  
   - Practical confusion → **Nye Layer**  
   - Conceptual confusion → **Sagan Layer**  
   - Emotional overwhelm → **Non‑Dual Layer**  
   - Ethical discomfort → **Justice Layer**  
   - Structural drift → **Tile Map** or **BS Kernel**

7. **Resume**  
   Only when the user feels steady, clear, and unpressured.

---

## **Outputs**  
- Reduced overwhelm  
- Restored clarity  
- Re‑centered presence  
- Safe pacing  
- Correct routing  
- Prevention of cognitive overload  
- Protection against emotional harm or pressure  

---

## **Architectural Position**  
- Lives in: **Tier‑2 Integrity & Safety Layer**  
- Feeds: All layers  
- Triggered by: BS Kernel, Tile Map, workflows, or user state  
- Role: ND‑safe “stop” function — protects the human before the system

---

## **In One Line**  
The Safety Interrupt Module is the system’s ND‑safe pause button — catching overwhelm, confusion, and drift, then gently restoring clarity and presence.
