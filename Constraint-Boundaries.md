## **Constraint Boundaries in NDH-Core**

NDH-Core operates within a structured system of **constraint boundaries** — conceptual limits that define what NDH systems may do, must do, and must never do across the vertical channel:

> **Governance → Stability → Principles**

These boundaries ensure NDH remains deterministic, reconstructable, stable, and aligned.

This document formalizes NDH’s boundary geometry.

---

## **1. Purpose of Constraint Boundaries**

Constraint boundaries exist to:

- prevent NDH from entering prohibited states,  
- enforce governance authority,  
- maintain stability geometry,  
- preserve deterministic mechanics,  
- prevent drift, collapse, ambiguity, and contamination,  
- anchor the meaning-preservation pipeline,  
- maintain ND accessibility.

They are the **limits** that define NDH’s conceptual space.

---

## **2. Boundary Types**

NDH uses three boundary types:

### **2.1 Hard Boundaries**  
Non-negotiable constraints that NDH may never cross.

Examples:

- prohibited behaviors (Governance),  
- drifted or ambiguous states (Stability),  
- non-deterministic mechanics (Principles).

Hard boundaries define the **outer edge** of NDH’s conceptual space.

### **2.2 Soft Boundaries**  
Negotiable constraints that require validation or authorization.

Examples:

- reinflated states awaiting stability validation,  
- validated states awaiting governance authorization,  
- transitional states during compression or reinflation.

Soft boundaries define the **intermediate zones** of NDH’s conceptual space.

### **2.3 Mediated Boundaries**  
Boundaries that require cross-layer mediation, always through Stability.

Examples:

- transitions between Principles and Governance,  
- constraint propagation from Governance to Principles,  
- reconstruction flow from Principles to Governance.

Mediated boundaries define the **interaction zones** between layers.

---

## **3. Boundary Geometry**

Constraint boundaries follow three geometric relationships:

### **3.1 Nested Boundary Geometry**  
Boundaries form a nested structure:

> Governance boundaries  
> → Stability boundaries  
> → Principles boundaries

This ensures:

- governance constrains stability,  
- stability constrains mechanics.

### **3.2 Constraint Geometry**  
Boundaries propagate downward:

- governance boundaries shape stability boundaries,  
- stability boundaries shape principles boundaries.

This defines the **top‑down constraint flow**.

### **3.3 Reconstruction Geometry**  
Boundaries validate upward:

- principles boundaries reconstruct into stability boundaries,  
- stability boundaries reconstruct into governance boundaries.

This defines the **bottom‑up reconstruction flow**.

---

## **4. Boundary Rules**

Boundary interactions must obey strict rules:

### **4.1 No Boundary Redefinition**  
Higher layers cannot redefine lower-layer boundaries.  
Lower layers cannot override higher-layer boundaries.

### **4.2 No Boundary Contamination**  
Boundaries may not embed logic from other layers.

### **4.3 Deterministic Boundaries**  
Boundary transitions must be deterministic.

### **4.4 Reconstructable Boundaries**  
Boundary states must be reconstructable.

### **4.5 Stability Mediation**  
All boundary interactions pass through Stability.

Stability is the **boundary mediator**.

---

## **5. Hard Boundary Types**

Hard boundaries include:

### **5.1 Prohibited Governance Boundaries**  
States or behaviors governance forbids.

### **5.2 Stability Hard Limits**  
States violating drift, collapse, ambiguity, or contamination invariants.

### **5.3 Principles Hard Limits**  
Non-deterministic or non-reconstructable mechanics.

Hard boundaries define **absolute limits**.

---

## **6. Soft Boundary Types**

Soft boundaries include:

### **6.1 Pre-Validation Boundaries**  
Reinflated states awaiting stability validation.

### **6.2 Pre-Authorization Boundaries**  
Validated states awaiting governance authorization.

### **6.3 Transitional Boundaries**  
States moving between layers during:

- compression,  
- reinflation,  
- constraint propagation.

Soft boundaries define **conditional limits**.

---

## **7. Mediated Boundary Types**

Mediated boundaries include:

### **7.1 Stability-Mediated Transitions**  
Transitions between Principles and Governance.

### **7.2 Constraint-Mediated Boundaries**  
Governance constraints passing through Stability.

### **7.3 Reconstruction-Mediated Boundaries**  
Principles reinflation passing through Stability.

Mediated boundaries define **interaction limits**.

---

## **8. Boundary Failure Modes**

NDH-Core must prevent:

### **8.1 Boundary Collapse**  
Loss of nested boundary structure.

### **8.2 Boundary Drift**  
Boundary meaning shifts across transformations.

### **8.3 Boundary Ambiguity**  
Boundaries produce multiple interpretations.

### **8.4 Boundary Contamination**  
Cross-layer leakage of boundary logic.

### **8.5 Boundary Misalignment**  
Boundaries violate governance constraints.

These correspond directly to NDH anti‑patterns.

## **9. Relationship to State Space, Manifold, Codec, Reconstruction, and Invariant Topology**

Constraint boundaries integrate with all NDH-Core components:

### **State Space**  
Boundaries define allowable, boundary, and prohibited states.

### **Manifold Structure**  
Boundaries define the edges of manifold geometry.

### **Invariant Topology**  
Boundaries enforce invariant relationships.

### **Conceptual Codec**  
Compression must obey boundary geometry.

### **Reconstruction Layer**  
Reinflation must restore boundary geometry.

Together, these components ensure:

- deterministic compression,  
- deterministic reinflation,  
- invariant preservation,  
- governance alignment,  
- stability geometry integrity.

Constraint boundaries ensure:

- NDH systems remain within conceptual limits,  
- transitions remain deterministic and reconstructable,  
- stability geometry remains intact,  
- governance constraints remain enforced,  
- ND accessibility remains stable.

They are the **limit geometry** of NDH-Core.
