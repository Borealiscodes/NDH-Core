## **Purpose**
The Two‑Pin System (TPS) defines how UMA anchors stability across both the **conceptual architecture** and the **governance integrity layer**.  
TPS ensures that every canonical module has:

- a **Map Pin** (structural anchor)  
- a **SIAP Pin** (integrity anchor)

This dual‑anchor model prevents conceptual drift, governance drift, premature cosmic anchoring, and viewport collapse.

---

## **Scope**
TPS applies to all UMA modules that:

- serve as navigational instruments  
- define architectural boundaries  
- influence safety or cognitive load  
- participate in multi‑layer cosmic structures  
- require canonical stability

---

## **Pin Types**
### **1. Map Pin — Structural Anchor**  
Anchors the module inside the conceptual architecture.  
Defines the module’s canonical role and prevents drift.

**Directory:**  
`/conceptual-engine/visuals/<module>/pins/`

### **2. SIAP Pin — Integrity Anchor**  
Anchors the module inside the SIAP governance ledger.  
Records approval, pacing, accessibility, and safety alignment.

**Directory:**  
`/conceptual-engine/safety/SIAP/logs/`

---

## **Why TPS Exists**
UMA operates across multiple conceptual planes:

- Ethics Core  
- Astrolabe (Navigation)  
- Heliosphere (Containment)  
- Dyson Sphere (Infrastructure)  
- Luna Base (Development)  
- Cosmic Forest (Meta‑Index)  
- Thoroughfare (Traversal)  
- SIAP (Governance)

Anchoring on only one plane creates instability.  
TPS ensures **dual stability** across both architecture and governance.
