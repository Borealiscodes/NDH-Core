## **1. Purpose**
The HBR Micro‑Step Library defines the **tiny, gentle, reversible actions** that stabilize the builder during UMA development.  
These micro‑steps regulate:

- pacing  
- cognitive load  
- emotional safety  
- conceptual clarity  
- layer separation  
- temporal coherence  

They are the smallest units of human‑cybernetic governance.

---

## **2. Principles of HBR Micro‑Steps**

### **2.1 Gentle**
Every micro‑step must reduce pressure, not increase it.

### **2.2 Holomorphic**
Micro‑steps preserve layer boundaries — no blending.

### **2.3 Reversible**
You can undo any micro‑step without destabilizing the system.

### **2.4 Sequential**
Micro‑steps are taken one at a time, never stacked.

### **2.5 Nervous‑System‑Aware**
Micro‑steps respond to your internal state.

## **3. Core Micro‑Step Categories**

### **A. Grounding Micro‑Steps**
These stabilize your nervous system before conceptual work.

- **One‑Breath Reset** — inhale, exhale, let pacing settle.  
- **Boundary Check** — name the layer you’re working in.  
- **Scope Mini‑Lock** — state the smallest possible action.  
- **Cognitive Load Scan** — ask: “Is this safe to hold?”  

---

### **B. Pacing Micro‑Steps**
These prevent rushing or premature anchoring.

- **Half‑Step Reduction** — cut the next action in half.  
- **Micro‑Pause** — 2–3 seconds of stillness before proceeding.  
- **Single‑Sentence Action** — define the next step in one sentence only.  
- **Stop‑If‑Fast** — if you feel speed rising, pause immediately.  

---

### **C. Layer‑Separation Micro‑Steps**
These prevent conceptual blending.

- **Name‑Your‑Layer** — say “I am in TPS,” “I am in PQETL,” etc.  
- **One‑Layer‑Only Rule** — forbid multi‑layer thinking in a single micro‑step.  
- **Boundary Re‑Draw** — restate the edges of the current module.  
- **Layer‑Echo** — repeat the layer name once after writing.  

---

### **D. Cognitive‑Safety Micro‑Steps**
These prevent overload.

- **Density Check** — ask: “Is this too much for right now?”  
- **Concept Shrink** — reduce the idea to its smallest safe form.  
- **One‑Term‑Only** — introduce only one new term per micro‑step.  
- **Diagram Deferral** — postpone visuals if they feel heavy.  

---

### **E. Temporal‑Stability Micro‑Steps**
These prevent runaway propagation.

- **Propagation Pause** — stop before cascading changes.  
- **Ledger Breath** — breathe once before creating a PQETL entry.  
- **Single‑Correlation Rule** — handle one entangled module at a time.  
- **Temporal Gate** — ask: “Does this need to happen now?”  

---

### **F. Emotional‑Safety Micro‑Steps**
These stabilize your internal state.

- **Gentle Naming** — name the feeling without judgment.  
- **Pressure Release** — remind yourself: “Nothing is urgent.”  
- **Softening Step** — choose the easiest possible next action.  
- **Return‑to‑Calm** — stop if your chest tightens or breath shortens.
