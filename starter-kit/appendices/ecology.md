# Ecology  
### The Living, Breathing Environment of Your Project

## 1. Purpose
The Ecology describes the soft, interconnected environment in which your project lives.  
It explains how ideas grow, how components interact, and how stability is maintained across the whole ecosystem.

Ecology is the gentle life of the project.

---

## 2. Principle: Everything Is Interconnected
Every part of the project influences every other part — softly.

Guidelines:
- connections are gentle  
- interactions are breathable  
- relationships are clear  
- motion is predictable  

Interconnection is not entanglement.

---

## 3. Principle: Growth Is Organic
Growth should feel natural, slow, and non-coercive.

This means:
- ideas emerge softly  
- documents expand gradually  
- boundaries adapt gently  
- workflows evolve without pressure  

Organic growth prevents collapse.

---

## 4. Principle: Diversity of Materials
The project thrives on a variety of materials.

Examples:
- core definitions  
- workflows  
- boundaries  
- stability rules  
- raw notes  
- extended references  

Diversity enriches the ecosystem.

---

## 5. Principle: Balance
The ecology stays healthy when components remain balanced.

Guidelines:
- no component dominates  
- no workflow overwhelms  
- no boundary restricts too tightly  
- no document grows too large  

Balance keeps the project breathable.

---

## 6. Principle: Gentle Cycles
The project moves through soft cycles.

Examples:
- stillness → noticing → action → review → stillness  
- seed → fragment → stroke → document → integration  
- idea → boundary → behavior → stability → idea  

Cycles keep the ecology alive.

---

## 7. Principle: Soft Edges
Edges between components should be gentle.

This means:
- clear boundaries  
- predictable transitions  
- breathable interfaces  
- no abrupt shifts  

Soft edges protect stability.

---

## 8. Principle: Environmental Stability
The ecology remains stable through:

- coherence rules  
- consistency guidelines  
- pacing practices  
- non-coercive boundaries  
- gentle workflows  

Stability is the climate of the project.

---

## 9. Principle: Regeneration
The ecology supports regeneration.

Examples:
- ideas return in new forms  
- documents evolve gently  
- boundaries refine themselves  
- workflows adapt over time  

Regeneration keeps the project alive.

---

---

## 11. Soft Failure Modes
If the ecology becomes unstable, failure should be gentle.

Examples:
- pause  
- simplify  
- remove one dependency  
- return to stillness  
- reintroduce components slowly  

Soft failure prevents collapse.

---

## 12. Summary
The Ecology describes the living environment of your project.  
It keeps growth gentle, interactions breathable, and stability intact.
