# Pacing Guide  
### A Gentle, Bob-Ross-Style Approach to Working With Your Project

## 1. Purpose
This guide helps you work at a pace that feels calm, steady, and sustainable.  
It protects your energy, your clarity, and your joy.  
Pacing is part of the architecture — not an afterthought.

This document is friendly, non-coercive, and supportive.

---

## 2. Principle: Your Pace Is the Correct Pace
Whatever pace you choose is the right one.  
Fast is fine.  
Slow is fine.  
Stopping is fine.  
Pausing is fine.

Your pace is part of the design.

---

## 3. Principle: Stop When You Need To
Stopping is not failure.  
Stopping is a structural choice.  
It keeps the project gentle, stable, and non-collapsing.

Stop whenever you feel the need.  
The project will wait for you.

---

## 4. Principle: Pause Often
Pausing lets ideas settle.  
It lets clarity rise.  
It keeps overwhelm away.

Pausing is a happy little breath in the workflow.

---

## 5. Principle: Work in Small Strokes
Small strokes keep the project manageable and joyful.  
One line at a time.  
One idea at a time.  
One gentle step.

Small strokes make big beauty.

---

## 6. Principle: Avoid Urgency
Urgency collapses pacing.  
Urgency creates pressure.  
Urgency makes the canvas feel tight.

This project avoids urgency entirely.

---

## 7. Principle: Respect Low-Spoons Days
If your energy is low, the project adapts.  
You can:
- do one tiny stroke  
- read one line  
- rename one file  
- or do nothing at all  

Low-spoons days are part of the architecture.

---

## 8. Principle: Celebrate Small Progress
Every tiny stroke counts.  
Every gentle addition matters.  
Every small improvement is a victory.

Celebrate the little things.

---

## 9. Principle: Stop Early
Bob Ross rule:  
Stop before you think you’re finished.

Leaving space is part of the art.  
It keeps the project breathable and joyful.

---

## 10. Principle: Return to Stillness
Stillness is where ideas arrive.  
Stillness is where clarity grows.  
Stillness is part of the workflow.

Return to stillness whenever you want.

---

## 11. Summary
This pacing guide keeps your project gentle, stable, and joyful.  
It protects your energy and supports your creativity.
