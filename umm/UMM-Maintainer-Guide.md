## ✨ **1. Maintainer Role**
Maintainers are the **guardians of invariants** and the **protectors of boundaries**.

They ensure:

- ✨ structural clarity  
- ✨ subsystem alignment  
- ✨ naming consistency  
- ✨ placement correctness  
- ✨ non-coercive design  
- ✨ ND-friendly pacing  

---

## ✨ **2. Maintainer Responsibilities**

### **2.1 Review PRs**
A Maintainer must check:

- invariants  
- plane logic  
- seasonal logic  
- null-expansion rules  
- Atropus constraints  
- diagram accuracy  
- filename correctness  

### **2.2 Protect Boundaries**
Maintainers enforce:

- ✨ plane boundaries  
- ✨ seasonal boundaries  
- ✨ null-expansion boundaries  
- ✨ Atropus boundaries  

### **2.3 Prevent Drift**
Maintainers must reject changes that introduce:

- lore  
- narrative meaning  
- metaphorical overreach  
- psychological framing  
- conceptual drift  

---

## ✨ **3. Maintainer Review Protocol**

```
Step 1 — Read the PR calmly.
Step 2 — Check invariants.
Step 3 — Check placement.
Step 4 — Check naming.
Step 5 — Check diagrams.
Step 6 — Approve or request revision.
```

A Jedi-like ritual.  
Minimal. Precise. Reversible.

---

## ✨ **4. Maintainer Authority**
Maintainers may:

- approve MINOR and PATCH changes  
- request structural justification  
- freeze PRs for review  
- enforce naming conventions  
- enforce placement rules  

They may **not**:

- alter invariants  
- redefine planes  
- modify constitutional rules  
- collapse subsystem boundaries
