## **NDH Manifold Structure**

NDH-Core operates on a conceptual manifold — a structured geometric space that defines how meaning, invariants, constraints, and reconstruction interact across the vertical channel:

> **Governance → Stability → Principles**

This manifold is not spatial, mathematical, or physical.  
It is **conceptual geometry**: the structured topology of NDH’s reasoning space.

The manifold ensures NDH systems remain deterministic, stable, aligned, and reconstructable.

---

## **1. Purpose of the Manifold**

The NDH manifold exists to:

- provide a geometric substrate for invariants,  
- define how layers interlock without contamination,  
- support deterministic compression and reinflation,  
- anchor stability geometry,  
- enforce governance boundaries,  
- maintain ND accessibility,  
- prevent drift, collapse, and ambiguity.

It is the **conceptual space** in which NDH operates.

---

## **2. Manifold Layers**

The manifold consists of three nested layers:

### **2.1 Principles Manifold**  
Defines NDH’s deterministic mechanics:

- stateless reasoning  
- invariant enforcement  
- reconstructable transformations  
- ND accessibility  
- drift resistance  

This is the **base manifold** — the foundation of NDH geometry.

### **2.2 Stability Manifold**  
Defines NDH’s stability geometry:

- drift prevention  
- collapse prevention  
- ambiguity prevention  
- contamination prevention  

This manifold overlays the Principles manifold and constrains it.

### **2.3 Governance Manifold**  
Defines NDH’s authority geometry:

- allowed behaviors  
- prohibited behaviors  
- escalation pathways  
- compliance boundaries  

This manifold overlays Stability and constrains it.

---

## **3. Manifold Geometry**

The NDH manifold is defined by three geometric relationships:

### **3.1 Nested Geometry**  
Each manifold contains the one below it:

> Governance  
> → Stability  
> → Principles

This containment ensures:

- authority constrains stability,  
- stability constrains mechanics.

### **3.2 Constraint Geometry**  
Constraints propagate downward:

- governance constraints shape stability,  
- stability constraints shape principles.

This defines the **top‑down constraint flow**.

### **3.3 Reconstruction Geometry**  
Meaning reconstructs upward:

- principles reconstruct into stability,  
- stability reconstructs into governance.

This defines the **bottom‑up reconstruction flow**.

---

## **4. Manifold Rules**

The manifold obeys strict rules:

### **4.1 No Redefinition Across Layers**  
Higher layers cannot redefine lower layers.  
Lower layers cannot override higher layers.

### **4.2 No Contamination Across Layers**  
Manifold content cannot leak across layers:

- mechanics cannot contain governance logic,  
- stability cannot contain codec mechanics,  
- governance cannot contain stability geometry.

### **4.3 Deterministic Geometry**  
All manifold interactions must be deterministic.

### **4.4 Reconstructable Geometry**  
All manifold states must be reconstructable.

### **4.5 Stability Mediation**  
All manifold interactions pass through Stability.

Stability is the **geometric mediator**.

---

## **5. Manifold Interaction Modes**

Manifold layers interact through three modes:

### **5.1 Reinforcement Mode**  
Invariants reinforce each other across layers.

Example:  
Determinism (Principles) reinforces Drift Prevention (Stability).

### **5.2 Constraint Mode**  
Higher layers constrain lower-layer behavior.

Example:  
Prohibited Behaviors (Governance) constrain Reinflation (Principles).

### **5.3 Validation Mode**  
Lower layers validate higher-layer constraints.

Example:  
Reconstructability (Principles) validates Governance Authorization.
