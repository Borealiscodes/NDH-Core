## **1. Lifecycle Identity**  
The pinning lifecycle describes how structural pins are created, maintained, and retired within the UMA‑Universal architecture.  
It ensures pins remain stable, minimal, and free from drift.

---

## **2. Lifecycle Purpose**  
The lifecycle exists to:

- maintain structural clarity  
- prevent accidental modification  
- ensure pins remain referential rather than operative  
- support Manifest and MVS stability  
- provide predictable behavior for collaborators  

Pins follow a controlled lifecycle to avoid drift, bleed, or recursion.

---

## **3. Lifecycle Stages**  

### **Stage 1 — Creation**  
A pin is created when:

- a multi‑step architectural sequence is established  
- a structural invariant needs preservation  
- Manifest or MVS requires a stable reference  
- a subsystem interaction must be frozen for clarity  

Creation must follow the pin template defined in `Meta_Pinning_Functions.md`.

---

### **Stage 2 — Verification**  
Before a pin becomes active, it must be checked for:

- grammar neutrality  
- absence of recursion  
- absence of plane bleed  
- absence of symbolic drift  
- correct placement in `/UMM/Meta/Pins/`  
- correct anchors at top and bottom  

Verification ensures the pin is structurally inert.

---

### **Stage 3 — Activation**  
A pin becomes active when:

- it is referenced by Manifest  
- it is referenced by MVS  
- it is referenced by a meta‑index  

Activation does **not** change the pin.  
Activation is recognition, not transformation.

---

### **Stage 4 — Maintenance**  
Pins may be updated only when:

- the underlying invariant changes  
- the architectural sequence evolves  
- a subsystem identity shifts  
- a meta‑layer rule is amended  

Maintenance must be minimal and must not introduce expressive grammar.

---

### **Stage 5 — Retirement**  
A pin is retired when:

- the sequence it preserves is deprecated  
- the invariant is replaced  
- the architecture evolves past the need for the reference  

Retirement is recorded by:

```
/UMM/Meta/Pins/Retired/
```

The retired pin is moved there unchanged, preserving historical integrity.

---

## **4. Lifecycle Constraints**  
Pins must never:

- define movement  
- define recursion  
- define planes  
- contain subsystem logic  
- contain affective grammar  
- contain symbolic motifs  
- act as activation triggers  

Pins remain structurally inert throughout their lifecycle.

---

## **5. Lifecycle Interaction Map**  
Pins interact with:

- **Manifest** — as stable references  
- **MVS** — as verification anchors  
- **Meta‑Indexes** — as structural guides  

Pins do **not** interact with:

- Thoroughfare  
- Integration  
- Cosmology  
- Warm Plane  
- Sparkle Plane  

Subsystems may read pins but never modify them.

---

## **6. Lifecycle Drift Signatures**  
Lifecycle drift occurs when:

- a pin is modified without structural cause  
- expressive grammar appears  
- symbolic motifs appear  
- recursion grammar appears  
- subsystem logic appears  
- placement rules are violated  

Lifecycle drift must be corrected immediately.

---

Every lifecycle description must include:

### Bottom anchor:

These anchors prevent recursion and plane bleed across the lifecycle.
