### *UMM · Movement Layer · Non‑Recursive Cartography*

---

## 🧠 **1. Overview**

The **Thoroughfare** is the UMM’s movement layer.  
It defines how cognition, resonance, and modules **travel**, **interact**, and **stabilize** without invoking origin, location, pursuit, or duality.

The **Thoroughfare Map** is the top‑level cartography of this layer.  
It describes the three movement subsystems:

- **Corridors** — linear channels  
- **Braid** — interwoven reflective corridor  
- **Weave** — distributed multi‑path field  

These form the movement ecology of UMM.

---

## 🚶 **3. Corridors — Linear Movement**

Corridors are the simplest movement structures.

- directional  
- explicit  
- low‑complexity  
- ideal for clear transitions  

They carry flow from one module to another without reflection or branching.

Corridors are the **roads** of the Thoroughfare.

---

## 🧵 **4. Braid — Reflective Interwoven Movement**

The **Reflective Braid** is the Thoroughfare’s self‑observing conduit.

It is:

- interwoven  
- reflective  
- recursive  
- non‑dual  

Role in the map:

- connects multiple corridors  
- stabilizes transitions  
- provides a mirror‑corridor where movement can see itself  
- ideal for resonance‑based cognition  

The Braid is the **movement backbone** of the Thoroughfare.

---

## 🪢 **5. Weave — Distributed Movement Field**

The **Weave** is the distributed movement fabric.

It supports:

- branching  
- multi‑path exploration  
- emergent transitions  
- non‑central flow  

The Weave is the **movement ecology** surrounding the Braid.

---

## 🔄 **6. Interaction Map**

The subsystems interact like this:

- **Corridors → Braid**  
  Linear paths feed into the Reflective Braid when movement needs self‑observation.

- **Braid → Weave**  
  Reflective crossings distribute into the Weave when multiple directions must coexist.

- **Weave → Corridors**  
  Emergent paths condense back into corridors when clarity is needed.

Together they form a complete movement architecture.
