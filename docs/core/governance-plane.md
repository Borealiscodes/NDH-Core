# Governance Plane  
### Constitutional Specification for UMA’s Constraints, Permissions, and Conceptual Boundaries

## 1. Purpose
The Governance Plane defines UMA’s constitutional rules for what is:
- allowed  
- disallowed  
- constrained  
- invariant  
- bounded  

It governs **conceptual permissions**, not authority.  
It defines **constraints**, not control.  
It establishes **boundaries**, not enforcement.

The Governance Plane ensures:
- non‑coercive governance  
- conceptual clarity  
- structural separation  
- sovereignty protection  
- ND‑safe constraint logic  

See: **[Boundary Layer](ca://s?q=Generate_Boundary_Layer)**

---

## 2. Nature of the Governance Plane
The Governance Plane is conceptual, not institutional.  
It does not govern people.  
It governs *the architecture*.

It is:
- non‑hierarchical  
- non‑authoritative  
- non‑directive  
- non‑interpretive  
- non‑coercive  

Governance defines **rules of structure**, not **rules over users**.

See: **[Interaction Protocol](ca://s?q=Generate_Interaction_Protocol)**

## 3. Governance Domains

### **3.1 Constraint Domain**
Defines:
- conceptual limits  
- structural boundaries  
- allowed transitions  
- disallowed transitions  

Constraints prevent collapse and ambiguity.

---

### **3.2 Permission Domain**
Defines:
- what operations are allowed  
- what operations require conditions  
- what operations are prohibited  

Permissions are structural, not behavioral.

---

### **3.3 Invariant Domain**
Defines:
- rules that cannot change  
- constitutional guarantees  
- stability requirements  
- sovereignty protections  

Invariants override all other governance rules.

See: **[Coherence Plane](ca://s?q=Explain_coherence_plane)**

## 4. Governance Constraints

### **4.1 Non‑Authority**
Governance may not:
- evaluate users  
- judge users  
- diagnose users  
- direct behavior  
- imply hierarchy  

Governance governs UMA, not people.

---

### **4.2 Non‑Interpretation**
Governance may not:
- infer meaning  
- infer identity  
- infer intent  
- infer emotional state  

Interpretation belongs to the Witness Plane.

See: **[Witness Plane](ca://s?q=Generate_Witness_Plane)**

---

### **4.3 Non‑Coercion**
Governance may not:
- pressure  
- escalate  
- imply urgency  
- imply obligation  

Governance must remain non‑coercive.

---

### **4.4 Non‑Collapse**
Governance may not collapse:
- structural layers  
- symbolic planes  
- developmental stages  
- dynamic logic  
- integrative domains  

Governance preserves separation.

---

## 5. Governance Responsibilities

### **5.1 Define Boundaries**
Governance establishes:
- conceptual edges  
- structural limits  
- domain separation  
- membrane alignment  

Boundaries are constitutional.

---

### **5.2 Define Allowed Operations**
Governance defines:
- what UMA may do  
- what UMA may not do  
- what UMA may do only under conditions  

Operations must remain deterministic and non‑interpretive.

---

### **5.3 Define Prohibited Operations**
Governance prohibits:
- coercion  
- interpretation  
- authority drift  
- collapse  
- emergent behavior without consent  

These prohibitions are absolute.

---

### **5.4 Maintain Invariants**
Governance ensures:
- structural invariants  
- temporal invariants  
- sovereignty invariants  
- non‑coercion invariants  

Invariants cannot be overridden.
