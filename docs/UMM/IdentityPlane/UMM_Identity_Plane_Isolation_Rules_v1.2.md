# **3. Identity Plane Isolation Rules (v1.2)**

These rules define what **ID‑HBR may interact with**, **must avoid**, and **must be supervised by**.

---

## **Rule 1 — Traversal Isolation (TRV‑CHS‑OL → ID‑HBR)**  
**ID‑HBR must never be directly traversed.**

Traversal into the identity plane is prohibited except through SIAP‑approved classification paths.

- Prevents traversal drift  
- Prevents emergent subsystem formation  
- Prevents identity‑plane contamination  

Explore: **TRV‑CHS‑OL**

---

## **Rule 2 — Narrative Isolation (NAR‑PE → ID‑HBR)**  
**Narrative subsystems may influence identity modeling but may not directly couple to ID‑HBR.**

Narrative influence is **loose**, not structural.

- Prevents narrative drift  
- Prevents identity‑plane contamination  
- Preserves subsystem boundaries  

Explore: **NAR‑PE**

---

## **Rule 3 — Cognitive Adjacency Only (SYS‑CHS ↔ ID‑HBR)**  
**ID‑HBR may only maintain structural adjacency with SYS‑CHS.**

This adjacency is required for identity modeling but must not escalate into traversal or narrative coupling.

- Prevents subsystem drift  
- Preserves identity‑plane clarity  
- Maintains SID alignment  

Explore: **SYS‑CHS**

---

## **Rule 4 — Governance Supervision (GOV‑SIAP → ID‑HBR)**  
**SIAP must supervise all identity‑plane classification and adjacency.**

SIAP ensures:

- correct subsystem classification  
- correct isolation boundaries  
- correct adjacency mapping  
- correct drift detection  

Explore: **GOV‑SIAP**

---

## **Rule 5 — Drift Monitoring (GOV‑SAF → ID‑HBR)**

- naming drift

- propagation drift
