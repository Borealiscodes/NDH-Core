# Forest Horizon (UMA Boundary Layer)
The Horizon defines the outer conceptual boundary of the UMA Meta Forest.
It marks the limit of safe, coherent exploration and the point beyond which
concepts are considered "outside UMA" and must not be structurally integrated
without explicit review.

---

## 1. Purpose of the Horizon
- **Boundary:** Establishes where UMA ends and external systems begin.
- **Containment:** Prevents uncontrolled expansion of maps, layers, and lore.
- **Clarity:** Distinguishes UMA-native concepts from adjacent or speculative ones.
- **Safety:** Protects cognitive load by limiting horizon-level operations.

The Horizon is not a sky, canopy, or path. It is the edge.

---

## 2. Horizon Rules
- **No direct structural binding** to concepts beyond the Horizon
  without an explicit, logged decision in `forest_roots.md`.
- **No new orbital maps** may be added that extend beyond the Horizon
  without revising this document.
- **No safety primitives** (Pause Layer, Clearing, Memorial) may be
  bypassed for horizon-level work.
- **All horizon-level experiments** must be:
  - clearly labeled as experimental
  - logged in `forest_roots.md`
  - referenced from `forest_growth.md` if they affect expansion rules.

---

## 3. Relationship to Other Layers
- **Canopy & Sky:** Operate fully within the Horizon.
- **Paths & Growth:** May approach the Horizon but must not cross it
  without explicit, documented intent.
- **Remembrance Layer:** Always remains inside the Horizon; it is
  a grounding mechanism, not an expansion mechanism.
- **Meta-Meta Index:** Sits above the Horizon conceptually, defining
  the rules by which the Horizon itself may be changed.

---

## 4. Horizon Operations
Horizon-level operations include:
- defining new outer boundaries
- interfacing with external systems, frameworks, or universes
- introducing cross-ecosystem mappings
- exploring speculative or high-risk conceptual terrain

All such operations must:
- respect cognitive-safety constraints
- be reversible or clearly scoped
- avoid redefining UMA’s core identity without consensus

---

## 5. Beyond the Horizon
"Beyond UMA" is intentionally under-specified here.

Any work beyond the Horizon:
- is not part of UMA by default
- must be treated as adjacent, not internal
- may be referenced, but not structurally anchored, without review

The Horizon exists to keep UMA whole, gentle, and coherent.
