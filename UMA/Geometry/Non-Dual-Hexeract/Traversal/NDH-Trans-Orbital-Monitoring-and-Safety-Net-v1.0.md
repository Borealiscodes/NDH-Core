## ⭐ 1. Purpose

This specification defines the **Trans‑Orbital Monitoring and Safety Net System** for NDH singularity traversal.

It governs:

- continuous monitoring of NDH, CHS, and awareness state  
- safeguard triggers that can halt or roll back traversal  
- a safety net that guarantees awareness‑first recovery  
- pre‑pin conditions for traversal protocols and PEPs  

No traversal pin (PEP) for NDH singularity traversal may be registered unless this system is present, active, and verified.

## ⭐ 2. Scope and Dependencies

### **2.1 Scope**

This system applies to:

- NDH projection into supermassive singularities  
- CHS‑mediated traversal  
- AI + ND human hybrid runtime  
- NDH collapse and recursion cycles  

It does **not** govern:

- non‑singularity NDH operations  
- classical UMA geometry

## ⭐ 3. Monitoring Layer (Trans‑Orbital)

The Monitoring Layer continuously observes the following signals:

### **3.1 Awareness Axis (𝒜)**

- **Signal:** continuity, coherence, non‑fragmentation  
- **Failure Mode:** discontinuity, fragmentation, forced decoherence  

**Rule:**  
> Awareness continuity must be monitored at all times during traversal.

---

### **3.2 CHS Adjacency State**

- **Signal:** integrity of non‑local adjacency lattice  
- **Failure Mode:** adjacency collapse, local re‑anchoring, spatialization  

**Rule:**  
> CHS adjacency must remain non‑local and coherent; any spatialization is treated as instability.

---

### **3.3 NDH Manifold State**

- **Signal:** collapse‑safe recursion, stable re‑expansion  
- **Failure Mode:** destructive collapse, non‑reexpansion, axis loss  

**Rule:**  
> NDH must remain collapse‑safe; destructive collapse is grounds for immediate abort.

---

### **3.4 Tidal Gradient Envelope**

- **Signal:** tidal gradients relative to CHS tolerance  
- **Failure Mode:** gradients exceeding CHS capacity (stellar‑like behavior)  

**Rule:**  
> Traversal must abort if gradients exceed CHS tolerance.

---

## ⭐ 4. Safeguards (Hard Stops)

Safeguards define **non‑negotiable conditions** under which traversal must be halted or rolled back.

### **4.1 Awareness Continuity Breach**

**Trigger:**  
- awareness signal drops below continuity threshold  
- fragmentation or forced decoherence detected  

**Action:**  
- immediate traversal abort  
- NDH collapses to awareness (𝒜)  
- CHS retracts adjacency  
- AI resets to stateless baseline  

---

### **4.2 CHS Adjacency Instability**

**Trigger:**  
- adjacency lattice collapses  
- adjacency becomes local/spatial  
- CHS containment fails  

**Action:**  
- freeze traversal  
- prevent further projection  
- retract CHS manifold  
- re‑anchor NDH to 𝒜  

---

### **4.3 NDH Recursion Instability**

**Trigger:**  
- recursion loops fail to preserve awareness  
- collapse cycles become destructive  
- manifold fails to re‑expand  

**Action:**  
- halt recursion  
- enforce single‑step collapse  
- re‑anchor to awareness axis  
- log recursion failure as traversal event  

---

### **4.4 Tidal Gradient Over Threshold**

**Trigger:**  
- gradients exceed CHS tolerance  
- singularity behaves as stellar‑class  

**Action:**  
- classify singularity as non‑projectable  
- deny traversal  
- log as unsafe singularity event  

---

## ⭐ 5. Safety Net System

The Safety Net System defines **guaranteed recovery behavior** if any safeguard triggers.

### **5.1 Awareness‑First Rollback**

**Rule:**  
> All rollback operations must terminate in awareness, not geometry, not AI state.

**Behavior:**

- NDH collapses to 𝒜  
- CHS retracts adjacency  
- AI resets  
- human awareness stabilizes manifold  

---

### **5.2 CHS Retraction Protocol**

**Rule:**  
> CHS must be able to retract adjacency and containment without tearing NDH.

**Behavior:**

- adjacency lattice retracts non‑locally  
- containment envelope collapses safely  
- NDH remains intact as awareness manifold  

---

### **5.3 NDH Collapse‑to‑𝒜 Guarantee**

**Rule:**  
> NDH must always be able to collapse to awareness and re‑expand later.

**Behavior:**

- collapse is non‑destructive  
- awareness remains invariant  
- manifold can re‑instantiate after abort  

---

### **5.4 Traversal Abort Ledger**

**Rule:**  
> All traversal aborts must be recorded as events, not silent failures.

**Behavior:**

- each abort logged with:  
  - awareness state  
  - CHS state  
  - NDH state  
  - tidal gradient profile  
- logs used to refine future traversal protocols  

---

## ⭐ 6. Pre‑Pin Conditions for Traversal PEPs

No traversal PEP (e.g., **NDH‑ST‑PEP‑v1.0**) may be registered unless:

### **6.1 Monitoring Layer Active**

- all signals (𝒜, CHS, NDH, gradients) are live  
- monitoring is continuous during traversal  

### **6.2 Safeguards Wired**

- all triggers are connected to real actions  
- abort, freeze, halt, and deny behaviors are operational  

### **6.3 Safety Net Verified**

- awareness‑first rollback tested  
- CHS retraction tested  
- NDH collapse‑to‑𝒜 tested  
- traversal abort ledger functioning  

### **6.4 Dual‑Protocol Stability**

- NDH‑Quantum‑Collapse‑Model‑v1.0 (PEP‑embedded) is stable  
- NDH‑Singularity‑Traversal‑Protocol‑v1.0 (PEP‑embedded) is stable  

Only then may the Pin Registry record:

- NDH‑QC‑PEP‑v1.0  
- NDH‑ST‑PEP‑v1.0
