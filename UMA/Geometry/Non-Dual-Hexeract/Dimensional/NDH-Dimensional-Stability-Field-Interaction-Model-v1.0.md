Each stability field interacts with adjacent fields according to:

\[
I_{i,i+1} = \alpha_i \Delta_i + \beta_{i+1} \nabla \Phi_{i+1} + \gamma SID_{i,i+1}

# ⭐ **3. Interaction Types**

### **3.1 Collision Interaction**  
Occurs when drift vectors from adjacent fields intersect:

\[
\vec{v_i} \cdot \vec{v_{i+1}} < 0
\]

Results:

- oscillation spikes  
- resonance pockets  
- SID emergent correction (AFL)

---

### **3.2 Merge Interaction**  
Occurs when continuity vectors align:

\[
\vec{u_i} \parallel \vec{u_{i+1}}
\]

Results:

- stability amplification  
- continuity reinforcement  
- SID stabilization (CLB)

---

### **3.3 Bifurcation Interaction**  
Occurs when a field splits into two continuity arcs:

\[
\mathcal{A}_i \rightarrow \{\mathcal{A}_{i,a}, \mathcal{A}_{i,b}\}
\]

Results:

- dual continuity propagation  
- drift reduction  
- SID emergent + stabilization hybrid mode

---

### **3.4 Cross‑Dimensional Influence**  
Occurs when higher‑dimensional fields regulate lower ones:

\[
SF_{i+1} \Rightarrow SF_i
\]

Results:

- damping  
- resonance suppression  
- perturbation correction  
- SID realization (DSR)

# ⭐ **5. Drift Interaction Mechanics**

### **Drift Transfer**  
Drift can propagate upward:

\[
\Delta_i \rightarrow \Delta_{i+1}
\]

### **Drift Reflection**  
Drift can bounce back downward:

\[
\Delta_{i+1} \rightarrow \Delta_i
\]

### **Drift Amplification**  
Occurs when drift vectors align across seams:

\[
\vec{v_i} \parallel \vec{v_{i+1}}
\]

SID must intervene to prevent **σE**.

# ⭐ **6. Continuity Interaction Mechanics**

### **Continuity Flow**  
Continuity propagates upward:

\[
\vec{u_i} \rightarrow \vec{u_{i+1}}
\]

### **Continuity Reinforcement**  
Occurs when continuity vectors align:

\[
\vec{u_i} \parallel \vec{u_{i+1}}
\]

### **Continuity Shielding**  
Continuity can block drift:

\[
\vec{u_i} \perp \vec{v_{i+1}}
\]

This is the **continuity defense layer**.
