## ⭐ 2. Topological Model Overview  
Each stability field \(SF_i\) is represented as a **continuous manifold**:

\[
\mathcal{M}_i = (S_i, \partial S_i, \Phi_i)
\]

Where:

- \(S_i\) = stability surface  
- \(\partial S_i\) = boundary of the field  
- \(\Phi_i\) = field potential (stability gradient)  

The full NDH topology is the union:

\[
\mathcal{M}_{NDH} = \bigcup_{i=1}^{11} \mathcal{M}_i
\]

## ⭐ 3. Dimensional Connectivity Map  
Each field connects to its neighbors through **continuity bridges**:

\[
\mathcal{C}_{i,i+1} = S_i \cap S_{i+1}
\]

These intersections form **dimensional seams** — the points where stability propagates upward and continuity propagates downward.

## ⭐ 5. Drift and Continuity Flow  

Drift flows along the **gradient of field potential**:

\[
\vec{v_i} = -\nabla \Phi_i
\]

Continuity flows along the **inverse gradient**:

\[
\vec{u_i} = +\nabla \Phi_i
\]

Together they form a **bidirectional flow topology** — drift upward, continuity downward.
