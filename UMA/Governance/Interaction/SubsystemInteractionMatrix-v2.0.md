# **3. Interaction Rules (v2.0)**

## **3.1 Structural Rules**
- UMA Core defines all inheritance.  
- UMM provides subsystem envelopes.  
- Roots ensures continuity across updates.

**Key link:** **Roots Ledger**

---

## **3.2 Routing Rules**
- SIAP governs all traversal paths.  
- Adjacency Matrix defines allowed edges.  
- No subsystem may route without SIAP validation.

**Key link:** **SIAP Spine**

---

## **3.3 Traversal Rules**
- CHS‑OL executes traversal.  
- CHS geometry may be partial.  
- Temporal Traversal Rules define directionality.

**Key link:** **Temporal Traversal Rules**

---

## **3.4 Containment Rules**
- Play Engine enforces IC/OOC boundaries.  
- Bleed‑Control gates must pass.  
- Temporal Containment Protocol governs time‑indexed containment.

**Key link:** **Play Engine**

---

## **3.5 Ethical Rules**
- HRD overrides SIAP when human‑adjacent surfaces are involved.  
- HRD constraints apply to traversal, containment, and routing.

**Key link:** **HRD Layer**

---

## **3.6 Stability Rules**
- HBR audits must confirm subsystem stability.  
- Unstable subsystems cannot traverse or integrate.

**Key link:** **HBR Stability**

# **5. Why v2.0 Was Necessary**

### **5.1 UMA now has a spine**
The interaction map needed to reflect:

- structural continuity  
- temporal sequencing  
- containment gates  
- adjacency constraints  
- ethical overrides  
- stability audits  

### **5.2 Temporal traversal is now first‑class**
v1.0 did not include:

- time‑indexed containment  
- temporal drift prevention  
- reverse traversal rules  
- conditional activation gates  

v2.0 does.

### **5.3 Play Engine required expanded handling**
IC/OOC traversal is both:

- spatial (adjacency)  
- temporal (lifecycle)  

v2.0 integrates both.

### **5.4 Partial CHS geometry is now supported**
Traversal works even without full cube completion.

Because the spine works.
